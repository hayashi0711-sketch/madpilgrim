# SCENE / SERVE Design Direction

Last updated: 2026-06-18

## Core Idea

MAD Pilgrim is a fashion-forward screen-tourism and screen-to-table editorial
product.

It connects:

1. a work,
2. a specific scene,
3. the real filming or visual-model location,
4. food shown or referenced in the work,
5. a verified place where that food experience is available.

## Visual System

This direction is original and must not reference previous MAD about U or
discarded MAD Pilgrim mockups.

- Contemporary Japanese fashion editorial, movie storyboard, and culinary art
  direction.
- High-contrast display serif only for short editorial titles.
- Neo-grotesk sans serif for navigation and product controls.
- Monospaced type for evidence dates, scene codes, availability, and source
  metadata.
- Milk white, black ink, stainless-steel gray, oxblood red, cobalt accents,
  and herb green for available-now status.
- Corners between 0 and 3px.
- No parchment, shrine styling, beige domination, glassmorphism, glow, or
  rounded SaaS card grids.

## Main Screen

- Immediate editorial discovery workspace, not a marketing page.
- A selected scene photograph or location image is the primary visual.
- Works and media types are indexed like a storyboard or magazine issue.
- Location and food experience are shown as equal editorial subjects.
- A compact map is a utility layer, not the entire brand identity.
- Every result exposes verification type and last checked date.

## Work Screen

- Work title, type, release information, and official source.
- Scene timeline with location and food markers.
- Separate labels for:
  - filmed here,
  - visual model,
  - narrative setting,
  - mentioned only.
- Unverified relationships are never presented as facts.

## Scene / Location Screen

- Exact scene and relationship to the place.
- Evidence source and verification date.
- Access, restrictions, and local etiquette.
- One-click route and save actions.
- Connected food experiences appear as an editorial band.

## Screen-to-Table Screen

- Food as represented in the work.
- Character, episode, timestamp, and scene context.
- Experience relationship:
  - actual filming venue,
  - exact menu,
  - official collaboration,
  - model menu,
  - inspired menu,
  - regional equivalent.
- Availability window, price, reservation, and last verified date.
- Ended experiences remain useful editorial records but are clearly marked.

## Product Language

Primary Japanese framing:

- 映像ロケ地ツーリズム
- 映像を旅して、物語を食べる。
- このシーンは、どこで撮られた？
- あの料理は、今どこで食べられる？

Primary English framing:

- Filming-location tourism
- Travel through the scene. Taste the story.
- Where was this scene filmed?
- Where can you experience the food now?

## Non-Negotiable Trust Signals

- Relation type.
- Verification status.
- Evidence source.
- Last checked date.
- Availability status.
- No automated publishing.
